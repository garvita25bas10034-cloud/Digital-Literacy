# TASK 5 – CYBERCRIME AWARENESS CASESTUDY & PREVENTION GUIDE

## PART B – Prevention Checklist

- Do not post sensitive personal information (Aadhaar number, phone number, address) on social media.  
- Set your social media profiles to “Friends Only” or private to limit public visibility.
- Back up important files regularly to a trusted cloud service or external drive.
- Stay aware and informed by following credible cybersecurity sources to keep up with new threats and scams.
- Always use strong, unique passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols for every account.  
- Avoid using names, birthdays, or continuous numbers while creating a password.
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all possible platforms.
- Install ad-blockers to avoid fake ads leading to phishing sites.
- Disable location services for apps that don’t require them.
- Verify job offers or prizes. Legitimate ones generally don’t ask for upfront payments.
- Block and report spam numbers on WhatsApp and phone.
- Clear browser cache and cookies monthly to prevent tracking.
- Use incognito mode for sensitive logins on shared devices.
- Be aware that UPI does not require a PIN to receive money.  
- It is a scam if someone asks you to enter your PIN to receive money.
- Always double-check the recipient’s UPI ID or phone number before confirming a transaction.
- Enable transaction alerts via SMS and email on all your bank accounts.
- If you are a victim of cybercrime, immediately contact:  
  - National Cyber Crime Portal – https://cybercrime.gov.in/  
  - 24/7 Helpline Number: 1930
